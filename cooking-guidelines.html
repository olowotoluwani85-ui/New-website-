Cooking Guidelines

1. Plan Ahead

  • Read the entire recipe before you begin.
  • Prep ingredients and measure everything first.
  • Assemble tools, cookware, and serving dishes in advance.

2. Keep it Clean

  • Wash your hands before cooking and after handling raw meat.
  • Clean surfaces and utensils between tasks.
  • Use separate cutting boards for raw proteins and produce.

3. Cook Safely

  • Keep hot pans, ovens, and knives under control.
  • Use oven mitts when handling hot cookware.
  • Never leave stovetop cooking unattended.

4. Balance Flavors

  • Taste food as you cook.
  • Use salt, acid, fat, and heat to enhance dishes.
  • Season gradually so you can adjust based on taste.

5. Manage Heat

  • Preheat pans before adding food.
  • Choose the right heat level for each ingredient.
  • Adjust temperature to avoid burning or undercooking.

6. Use Fresh Ingredients

  • Choose ripe produce and quality proteins.
  • Fresh herbs and citrus brighten dishes.
  • Avoid using expired or spoiled ingredients.

7. Rest and Serve

  • Let meat rest so juices redistribute.
  • Plate thoughtfully for appearance and temperature.
  • Serve food promptly while it is fresh and hot.

8. Learn and Improve

  • Try new recipes and cooking techniques regularly.
  • Note what worked well and what you would change next time.
  • Enjoy the process and stay curious in the kitchen.
